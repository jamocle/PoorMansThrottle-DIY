# Why Poor Man’s Throttle is Better for Traditional DC Layouts

## Modern Wireless Control for Traditional DC Model Railroads

**Run your transformer at full power — let PMT handle the rest.**

Poor Man’s Throttle brings modern high-torque PWM motor control to traditional DC layouts. Instead of slowing trains by reducing track voltage like a conventional transformer, PMT keeps the transformer at full output and electronically controls locomotive speed for smoother, stronger, and more consistent operation.

You keep the simplicity of track-powered DC, but gain the performance of modern electronic motor control and wireless operation.

## The Problem with Traditional DC Transformers

Traditional model train transformers control speed by **changing the track voltage**.

When you turn the knob:

* Low speed = **low voltage**
* High speed = **high voltage**

For example:

| Throttle Setting | Track Voltage                 |
| ---------------- | ----------------------------- |
| Slow crawl       | ~2–4 V                        |
| Medium speed     | ~8–12 V                       |
| Full speed       | ~18–24 V (depending on scale) |

This method works, but it creates a few **inherent problems**.

---

## Voltage Drop Across the Layout

Because traditional DC systems rely on **voltage level**, any resistance in the rails causes voltage loss.

Common causes include:

* Long track runs
* Rail joiners
* Dirty rail
* Small feeder wires
* Turnouts and switches

This results in **voltage drop** across the layout.

Typical symptoms:

* Train slows down far from the power feed
* Train speeds up near the transformer
* Inconsistent operation around the layout
* Locomotives stall at low speeds

This is why large layouts often require **many feeder wires and complex power districts**.

---

## Poor Low-Speed Torque

Another downside of voltage control is **low torque at low voltage**.

When the transformer outputs only a few volts:

* The motor receives very little power
* Torque is weak
* Locomotives struggle to start smoothly
* Slow-speed switching becomes difficult
* jerky starts
* poor slow-speed control
* weak pulling power
* locomotives that stall easily

This is why many locomotives **jerk when starting** or stall at slow speeds.

---

# The Poor Man’s Throttle Solution

Poor Man’s Throttle takes a **completely different approach**.

Instead of controlling trains by reducing voltage, the system uses **high-frequency PWM (Pulse Width Modulation)**.

### What this means

You run your transformer at **full track voltage**, and the PMT hardware handles speed control electronically.

Example:

Transformer → **Full Power Output** → PMT Controller → Track

The PMT then regulates motor speed using PWM.

---

## What is PWM?

PWM controls speed by rapidly switching the full voltage **on and off thousands of times per second**.

Instead of lowering voltage, it changes the **percentage of time the voltage is ON**.

Example:

| Throttle        | PWM Duty Cycle | Effective Power |
| --------------- | -------------- | --------------- |
| Slow crawl      | 10%            | Very slow speed |
| Switching speed | 30%            | Moderate        |
| Mainline speed  | 70%            | Fast            |
| Full throttle   | 100%           | Full power      |

But here’s the key advantage:

Even at low speeds, the motor receives **full-voltage pulses**.
