PMT Connectivity Explained

BLE and Wi-Fi working together.

PMT combines Bluetooth Low Energy, Wi-Fi, and WebSocket connectivity to deliver fast control, broader coverage, and advanced integration options in one flexible platform.

Expanded overview

PMT is built around two communication modes that work together to improve reliability and flexibility. Bluetooth Low Energy serves as the primary connection for direct control, giving users a fast and efficient way to communicate between a mobile device and PMT hardware. Wi-Fi acts as the secondary layer, extending communication across larger spaces and supporting more advanced network-based operation.

This dual-mode approach gives PMT a major advantage. Instead of relying on a single connection type for every situation, PMT can prioritize quick local responsiveness while also supporting broader coverage when a layout becomes larger, more complex, or harder to reach consistently. The result is smoother operation, fewer interruptions, and a control experience that scales better as needs grow.

Primary connection: BLE BLE provides responsive direct communication with low power use, low overhead, and excellent suitability for command-and-status traffic.
Secondary connection: Wi-Fi Wi-Fi adds longer reach, stronger full-layout coverage, and support for installations where broader network access is valuable.
Automatic failover PMT is designed to move between BLE and Wi-Fi more smoothly, helping reduce dropouts and maintain dependable control.
WebSocket interface Users can connect custom software, dashboards, websites, or programs directly to PMT for specialized control and integration.

Why BLE is better than classic Bluetooth for this use case

BLE, or Bluetooth Low Energy, is a better fit for PMT than classic Bluetooth because it is designed for lightweight, efficient communication rather than heavier continuous connections. Classic Bluetooth is commonly associated with things like speakers, headsets, and ongoing media streams. BLE is optimized for quick exchanges of compact data, which makes it much better suited for sending commands, receiving status updates, and maintaining responsive control. It also offers significantly better usable range for this application, with BLE reaching up to 150 meters while classic Bluetooth is often only around 30 feet.

Benefits of BLE for PMT

  • Longer range: BLE can reach up to 150 meters, giving PMT much more operating distance than classic Bluetooth, which is often only about 30 feet.
  • Lower power consumption: BLE is designed to use much less energy, which is ideal for compact onboard or control hardware.
  • Fast control messaging: It works extremely well for short, frequent packets such as throttle updates, direction changes, and function commands.
  • Lower connection overhead: BLE keeps communication lean and efficient rather than treating everything like a persistent high-bandwidth session.
  • Better for responsive control: PMT benefits from a connection type that is purpose-built for device control rather than media streaming.
  • Excellent mobile compatibility: Phones and tablets are already well suited for BLE-based control experiences.

Compared with classic Bluetooth

  • Classic Bluetooth is heavier: It was designed around different connection patterns, often involving more persistent and higher-bandwidth use cases.
  • It has much shorter range: In this context, classic Bluetooth is typically only about 30 feet, which is far less flexible than BLE’s longer reach.
  • It can be less efficient for control traffic: Small command packets do not need the same kind of connection behavior as audio or media devices.
  • It is often more than is needed: For direct control, a simpler and lighter communication model is usually the better choice.
  • BLE is the cleaner fit: It gives PMT an efficient local-control channel that aligns much better with the product’s job.
BLE gives PMT a more efficient local communication path than classic Bluetooth. It is better suited for precise control, lower power use, lightweight two-way status communication, and much greater usable range.

What the Wi-Fi side adds

Wi-Fi expands PMT beyond short-range direct communication. It supports broader layout coverage, makes operation more practical across larger spaces, and helps maintain connectivity in more demanding environments. That is especially important where distance, obstacles, benchwork, or tunnel sections can make direct short-range communication less ideal.

Wi-Fi also enables infrastructure-based connectivity. Extenders can be used to improve coverage through long tunnels or difficult areas, helping create a more seamless operating experience across the full railroad. For larger layouts, clubs, or installations where range matters, this is a major practical advantage.

In addition to coverage, Wi-Fi opens the door to software integration. PMT’s WebSocket interface allows websites, programs, and custom control tools to communicate directly with the system. That makes it possible to build custom throttles, monitoring dashboards, automation tools, or specialized interfaces around the PMT platform.

Flexible installation options

1. Between transformer and track PMT can be installed between the transformer and the track for a simple setup that works well with unmodified trains while still adding PMT control capabilities.
2. Onboard with track power PMT can also be installed inside the locomotive while the rails continue providing power, enabling onboard control of motor, lights, and functions with more individual control.
3. Deadrail with onboard battery PMT supports battery-powered onboard installations for maximum freedom, eliminating dependence on powered rails and enabling a more self-contained approach.
Why this matters These options make PMT adaptable to different operating styles, from simple retrofits to advanced onboard installations with a higher degree of independence and flexibility.

PMT is designed to deliver dependable communication, flexible installation, and room for future expansion. BLE provides efficient and responsive local control, Wi-Fi adds reach and infrastructure support, automatic failover helps reduce interruptions, and WebSocket access makes custom integrations possible for advanced users.
