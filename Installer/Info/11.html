Throttle Screen Improvement

Scrollable on smaller devices.

The throttle screen now supports vertical scrolling on compact mobile displays so the interface remains usable, readable, and comfortable instead of feeling compressed or visually squashed.

What changed

On smaller phones, there is less vertical space available to show every control at once. Previously, this could make the throttle experience feel cramped, with controls and status areas competing for limited room on the display. The updated behavior allows the screen to scroll vertically, which gives each section enough space to breathe while keeping the layout clear and easy to operate.

This change is focused on preserving usability. Instead of shrinking important interface elements until they are harder to read or interact with, the screen now flows naturally in a mobile-friendly vertical layout. Users can move through the screen smoothly and still access the same controls without sacrificing clarity.

Better readability Text, indicators, and controls are given more room, making the screen easier to understand at a glance.
Less visual crowding The interface no longer has to force everything into a tight space on shorter displays.
Improved touch comfort Buttons and interactive areas can remain more comfortable to use instead of being compressed.
Consistent experience Larger devices still feel spacious, while smaller devices now get a cleaner and more practical layout.
This is a user-experience improvement, not a feature reduction. The goal is to keep the throttle screen fully functional while making it more natural on devices where height is limited.

In practical terms, this means users on smaller mobile devices can scroll down through the throttle screen rather than dealing with a compressed presentation. That helps preserve the hierarchy of information, reduces the chance of missed controls, and makes the overall interaction feel more deliberate and polished.

The design here intentionally stays simple and refined, with a clean presentation that feels lightweight, modern, and optimized for mobile viewing.
