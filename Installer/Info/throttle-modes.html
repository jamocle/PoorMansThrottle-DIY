Throttle Screen Options

Three throttle views, each with a different feel.

The app includes Novice, Standard, and Pro throttle screens so the experience can match the operator. Whether someone wants simple control, a more realistic feel, or finer braking behavior, there is a view that fits.

Not every operator wants the same kind of throttle. Some users want a screen that is easy to understand in seconds. Others want more realism and more control. That is why the app offers three throttle styles instead of forcing one layout on everyone.

Each throttle view builds on the one before it. Novice is intentionally simple. Standard adds a more engaging sense of train handling through momentum and operational options. Pro goes further by introducing finer braking control for users who want a richer driving experience.

Novice Throttle

Simple, approachable, and easy to hand to anyone.

Novice mode is designed for users who do not need a lot of train control knowledge. The screen keeps the experience friendly and obvious, making it a great choice for a first-time user, a guest, or a child.

The goal of Novice mode is confidence. It removes unnecessary complexity and presents the core actions in a very direct way. Someone can look at it and quickly understand how to make the train move, change direction, and stop without needing deeper knowledge of more advanced throttle behavior.

Standard Throttle

A more advanced throttle with momentum and switching control.

Standard mode is more advanced than Novice and is meant to feel a little more fun and a little more realistic. It introduces the concept of momentum, giving throttle changes more weight and making train handling feel less immediate.

Momentum changes the feel of operation by preventing the train from reacting like a simple on-and-off toy. Instead, the throttle feels more alive and more deliberate. This makes Standard mode a nice step up for users who want more involvement without jumping all the way into the most advanced view.

Standard mode also introduces Switcher Mode. When enabled, Switcher Mode removes the heavy momentum effect so that controlled switching operations become easier and more precise. That gives the operator the flexibility to enjoy momentum during regular running, then switch to a more responsive feel when yard work or switching tasks require finer control.

Pro Throttle

Adds a real feather brake for finer control.

Pro mode builds on the Standard throttle and introduces a real feather brake. This is not just a simple on-and-off brake. It allows slight braking control, which makes operation feel more nuanced and more satisfying for users who want that extra layer.

The feather brake makes it possible to apply braking in a more subtle way instead of treating braking as a single binary action. That adds another level of fun and finesse to train handling, especially for users who enjoy making small adjustments while driving.

Pro mode is best for operators who want the richest control experience available in the app. It keeps the familiar layout ideas from the other screens while giving the user more expressive control over how the train behaves.

PIN Protection

Lock the throttle view to keep it child-friendly.

The app also supports a PIN that can lock out the ability to change the throttle screen view without entering the PIN. This is especially useful when the throttle is being used by children or guests and you want the current experience to stay exactly as you set it.

That means a child can stay in a simpler view like Novice without accidentally switching into a more advanced mode. It also helps parents and operators keep the interface consistent, predictable, and appropriate for the person holding the device.

PIN protection makes the throttle experience safer and more controlled for shared use, especially in family settings.
